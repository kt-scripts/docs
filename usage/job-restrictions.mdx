KT Extra Menu supports job-based access control. With it, you can restrict the menu to specific professions — e.g. mechanics, police or EMS — instead of making it available to all players. The entire logic is controlled by a single configuration value: `Config.RequiredJob`.

## Default behavior

By default, `Config.RequiredJob` is an **empty table**. This means: **every player** may open the menu, regardless of their job.

```lua
-- All players may use the menu (default)
Config.RequiredJob = {}
```

As soon as at least one job is in the list, access is restricted to exactly those jobs. Players with another job receive the message _"You don't have permission!"_.

## Configure job restrictions

### Allow only one job

```lua
-- Only mechanics may open the menu
Config.RequiredJob = {'mechanic'}
```

### Allow multiple jobs

```lua
-- Mechanics, police and EMS may open the menu
Config.RequiredJob = {'mechanic', 'police', 'ambulance'}
```

### Allow all players (default)

```lua
-- No restriction – anyone may use the menu
Config.RequiredJob = {}
```

<Note>
  Job names must **exactly** match the job names in your framework. For ESX, these are the `name` fields in the `jobs` database table; for QBCore/QBox, they are the keys in `QBShared.Jobs`. Case sensitivity and whitespace are taken into account.
</Note>

<Note>
  When `Config.Framework = 'standalone'` is used, job restrictions are **ignored** — every player is automatically considered authorized, regardless of the contents of `Config.RequiredJob`.
</Note>

## Driver only – Config.OnlyDriver

Independently of the job check, you can additionally restrict access to the **driver's seat**. When `Config.OnlyDriver = true` is set, only players in the driver's seat (seat -1) can open the menu. Passengers receive the message _"You must be the driver!"_.

```lua
Config.OnlyDriver = true  -- only the driver may open the menu
Config.OnlyDriver = false -- all occupants may open the menu (default)
```

This setting takes effect **before** the job check: if the player is not a driver, the menu is denied immediately, without even checking the job.

<Tip>
  Combine `Config.OnlyDriver = true` with a restricted `Config.RequiredJob` to enable the menu only for the driver of a specific service vehicle — for example only for the driver of a mechanic response vehicle:

  ```lua
  Config.OnlyDriver    = true
  Config.RequiredJob   = {'mechanic'}
  ```

  This way, neither passengers nor players of other professions can open the menu.
</Tip>
